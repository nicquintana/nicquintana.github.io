MCPL Reports Increase in Borrowed eMaterials

By Nicolas Quintana | May 8, 2023

Montgomery County Public Libraries (MCPL) has reported a significant increase in the number of borrowed electronic items from all branches in their system. The surge in demand for eBooks and eAudiobooks reflects the evolving reading habits and the growing popularity of digital content.

According to MCPL, the number of eMaterials, like eBooks and eAudiobooks, borrowed in the past couple of years has increased by 40% compared to years prior. This surge in demand is not limited to a specific age group, but it includes a wide range of readers, from children to seniors. The trend is seen across all 22 branches of MCPL, with some branches reporting an increase of over 50%.

The increase in borrowing electronic items from MCPL is not surprising, given the changes in reading habits brought about by the pandemic. With people spending more time at home, the demand for digital content has surged. In addition, the convenience of borrowing books without leaving one's home has become more appealing to readers. "I’ve gotten some eBooks from here a couple of times," said Britney Fru, a Montgomery County resident who frequents the Wheaton Library. "The library has a good variety of books to choose from.”

MCPL has been working to keep up with the increased demand for digital content. The library system has invested in expanding its collection of eBooks and eAudiobooks to offer more choices for readers. They have also increased the number of digital copies of popular titles to reduce wait times for patrons.

"Our most loaned materials are still physical books, particularly children's books, which makes sense since they are shorter, and most families check them out a few dozen at a time. Physical books are still most popular in the adult collection as well, but they are quickly being caught up to by our eBook and eAudiobooks, that many people access through their mobile devices," said Felicity Brown, the Collection Management Manager for MCPL.

The increased borrowing of electronic items from MCPL is part of a broader trend in the library world. According to the American Library Association, the demand for eBooks and eAudiobooks has grown steadily over the past decade. As a result, the digital circulation of public libraries has been increasing at a faster rate than the circulation of physical materials.

Despite the increase in demand for digital content, MCPL emphasizes the importance of maintaining a balance between electronic and physical resources. The library system continues to offer a wide variety of physical materials, including books, magazines, DVDs, and more. “We always encourage members of the community to make greater use of all of our resources,” said MCPL librarian Jeff Coster.

Brown also believes that more residents should take advantage of the resources the library system offers. "It's all free! Our eResources are available 24/7, even when our physical buildings are closed. And for our physical collection, if we don't own it in our library system, we're happy to borrow it from another library system for our customers if it's available. We have programs for all ages and interests, and the library is great way to learn something or try something new."

As the pandemic continues to reshape how we live and work, the demand for digital content will likely continue growing. MCPL and other libraries nationwide are poised to meet this demand, providing readers with access to high-quality digital resources and ensuring their libraries remain vibrant and relevant in the digital age.
